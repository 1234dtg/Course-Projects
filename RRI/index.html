Rogers Centre — Toronto (SkyDome)

Fully retractable — 4 panels
MechanismFour steel roof panels on circular/curved rail tracks; two main panels slide laterally, a third nests by rotating/sliding, and one panel is fixed. Heavy bogies and wheel assemblies carry ~11,000 tonnes of moving steel.
Materials / StructureSteel trusses, rail bogies, heavy-duty drive motors, weather seals.
Operation~20 minutes for full open/close. Sequenced drives with interlocks and braking systems.
Review / Adaptive featuresHistoric, true full open/close capability; best for extreme weather control but relatively slow and maintenance intensive.

Mercedes‑Benz Stadium — Atlanta

Aperture 'petal' roof — 8 panels
MechanismEight triangular petal panels act like a mechanical iris, sliding/pivoting inward along tracked guides. Synchronized servo drives and redundant motors ensure coordinated movement.
Materials / StructureSteel trusses, ETFE cushions in some sections, multi-motor drive systems, guide rails and stabilizing rails.
OperationFull cycle typically in ~8–12 minutes; precise synchronization and safety interlocks required.
Review / Adaptive featuresFast, iconic and highly flexible for quick weather/event changes. Complexity increases lifecycle maintenance and inspection demands.

SoFi Stadium — Los Angeles (Inglewood)

Fixed ETFE canopy with operable vents
MechanismFixed translucent ETFE canopy spanning the bowl with multiple operable vents; not a fully retractable roof.
Materials / StructureETFE membrane, cable-net support system, steel compression ring and space frame.
OperationNo full open/close cycle; vents operate for ventilation and light control.
Review / Adaptive featuresHybrid design gives shade and a semi-outdoor feel. Lower mechanical complexity than full retractables; passive ventilation and localized active controls for comfort.

National Stadium — Singapore (Sports Hub)

Two large sliding roof segments, ETFE cladding
MechanismTwo hydraulically/servo-driven roof segments slide on large bogies along the dome arch. Precision control systems handle movement in humid/tropical conditions.
Materials / StructureSteel trusses, ETFE cushions, sealed joints and dedicated drainage for tropical rainfall.
Operation~15–25 minutes for full transitions; integrated with ventilation & environmental control strategy.
Review / Adaptive featuresOptimized for tropical climate (sun/rain), lightweight ETFE lowers structural loads; good for rapid event reconfiguration though limited in partial‑opening granularity vs multi‑panel systems.

Sapporo Dome — Sapporo, Japan

Sliding roof + movable pitch
MechanismCombination of a retractable roof and a **mobile pitch** that can slide in/out of the stadium to expose natural turf to sunlight; roof sections allow enclosure when needed.
Materials / StructureSteel trusses, rail systems for both roof and pitch, hydraulic & motorized drives.
OperationField transfer and roof transitions are scheduled operations, taking significant time and coordination.
Review / Adaptive featuresExtremely versatile (sports conversion, climate control), but very complex and resource intensive to operate.

Fabric canopy + retractable pitch
MechanismTeflon-coated fabric roof with a large-span truss and retractable pitch that slides out. The fabric canopy can be partially retracted to create an open-air atmosphere.
Materials / StructureTeflon-coated fiberglass, steel trusses, sliding bogies for pitch.
OperationPitch slides out for concerts/maintenance; canopy transitions are less frequent and typically done for season changes.
Review / Adaptive featuresGreat multi-use venue; fabric roof reduces weight and enables large spans, though fabric requires careful maintenance for tensile integrity.

OperationRapid closure to avoid rain delays; designed for frequent switching during sports seasons.

OperationQuick closure (~10 minutes) to avoid rain interruptions to play.

T‑Mobile Park (formerly Safeco) — Seattle

Rolling panels on rails (true open-air)
MechanismThree large steel roof panels mounted on wheeled bogies that roll away on tracks to provide a genuine open-air field.
Materials / StructureSteel roof panels, multiple wheel assemblies (~96 wheels), drive motors and master PLC control.
OperationPanels roll away; cycle times vary but are designed for day-of-game operation with decent speed.
Review / Adaptive featuresProvides real outdoor experience when weather permits. Mechanically simpler than some other multi-petal systems, easing maintenance.
